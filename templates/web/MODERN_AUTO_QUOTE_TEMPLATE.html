What these coverages actually mean

Liability (50/100/50)

If an accident is your fault, this pays for the other people's injuries and property — up to $50K per person / $100K per accident for injuries, and $50K for property damage.

Comprehensive

Covers your vehicle for things that aren't a crash — theft, hail, fire, vandalism, or hitting a deer. You'd pay the deductible, insurance covers the rest.

Collision

Repairs or replaces your vehicle after a crash, no matter who's at fault. The deductible is what you pay before coverage kicks in.

Deductible

The part of a claim you pay yourself. A lower deductible means less out of pocket when something happens; a higher one usually lowers your premium.

Good to know: Your 50/100/50 liability meets North Carolina's required minimums for 2026. Want stronger protection or a different deductible? Just ask us — we'll re-quote it in minutes.
